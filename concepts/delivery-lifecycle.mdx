# Delivery Lifecycle

Every dispatch is tracked by a [Flight](/concepts/pigeons), and that flight moves through a small set of states. Understanding these transitions tells you exactly where a pigeon is and what — if anything — you need to do about it.

## The happy path

<Steps>

<Step title="Queued">

The pigeon is created and waiting on a perch at its [Aviary](/concepts/aviaries) for an open launch window. Status: `queued`.

</Step>

<Step title="In flight">

The pigeon launches and is en route along its chosen [Route](/concepts/routes), possibly taking a rest period or two on the scenic path. Status: `in_flight`.

</Step>

<Step title="Delivered">

The pigeon reaches the recipient and the flight closes successfully. Status: `delivered`. You'll receive a `flight.delivered` webhook if you've registered one.

</Step>

</Steps>

## When things go sideways

<Callout variant="warning">

Not every flight ends in delivery. A flight becomes **`lost`** if the pigeon never arrives — these auto-refund, no support ticket required (a courtesy the Ravens never offered). A flight becomes **`returned`** when the recipient is unreachable or you recall the dispatch; the pigeon comes home and no charge applies. Both are terminal states, so listen for them on your [Webhooks](/api-reference).

</Callout>

## State machine

```text
                +----------+
                |  queued  |
                +----------+
                     |
                     v
              +-------------+
        +-----|  in_flight  |-----+
        |     +-------------+     |
        |            |            |
        v            v            v
  +-----------+ +-----------+ +-----------+
  | delivered | |   lost    | | returned  |
  +-----------+ +-----------+ +-----------+
   (success)    (auto-refund) (unreachable
                               / recalled)
```

`queued`, `in_flight`, `delivered`, `lost`, and `returned` are the only flight states. `delivered`, `lost`, and `returned` are terminal. For polling and subscription patterns, see [tracking flights](/guides/tracking-flights).
